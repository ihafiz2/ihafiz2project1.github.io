Understanding the influence of biochemical markers and lifestyle choices on Heart Disease

Welcome to our detailed analysis of the influence of different common factors on heart disease through interactive visualization. This series dives into various aspects of cardiovascular health, focusing on key elements that affect heart disease risk and prevalence. Our goal is to provide a thorough analysis that not only informs but also empowers you with a deeper understanding of how factors like age, gender, and lifestyle impact heart health. By exploring these dimensions, we aim to equip you with valuable insights and help you make informed decisions regarding your health.

Scene 1: Cardiovascular Disease: Gender Population - Age, Physical Activity, Alcohol Intake & Smoking Habits

We begin our exploration by analyzing how the propensity of heart disease depends on age and how physical activity, alcohol intake, and smoking habits influence this trajectory. This scene uncovers the risk of heart disease with age and habits of smoking and alcohol intake and the crucial role of physical activity in mitigating these risks. By understanding these patterns, we can develop effective prevention strategies and tailor health recommendations to different stages of life. Our analysis based on cardiovascular dataset found on Kaggle. The data consists of 70,000 patient records, 34,979 presenting with cardiovascular disease while 35,021 without. There are 11 features 4 of them demographic, such as age, height, weight and gender, 4 of them biochemical markers, such as Systolic and Diastolic blood pressure data, Cholesterol and Glucose levels and 3 of them are physical habits, such as physical activity, smoking and alochol intake.

Scene 2: Cardiovascular Disease: Gender Poulation - Obesity Level

In this scene, we examine the impact of obesity levels on the propensity of heart disease across genders. By comparing the prevalence of heart disease between men and women, we highlight the effect of obesity specific to genders and its correlation between healthy individuals and patients with heart disease. This analysis underscores the critical role of obesity in heart disease risk and offers insights into potential prevention strategies.

Scene 3: Cardiovascular Disease: Gender Population - Glucose & Cholesterol Levels

In this scene, we investigate the effects of glucose and cholesterol levels on heart disease risk, focusing on gender differences. We provide a detailed analysis of how these key health indicators influence heart health and highlight the importance of monitoring these levels as part of a comprehensive health strategy. Learn how managing glucose and cholesterol can play a pivotal role in reducing heart disease risk.

This narrative visualization transcends mere statistics, offering a story of how various factors contribute to heart disease risk and how we can address them.
